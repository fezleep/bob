# brand

bob should feel personal, premium, and startup-ready without becoming loud.

The product should read as a calm tool made by someone with care for both
engineering and product taste. It should feel useful before it feels decorative.

## product philosophy

bob exists to make lead work clearer.

The product should help a team answer simple questions:

- who are we talking to?
- where does each conversation stand?
- what changed recently?
- what should happen next?

Every feature should support one of those questions. If a feature adds visual
weight, process, or explanation without improving the workflow, it should wait.

AI can help later, but it should stay quiet and attached to specific tasks:
summaries, next steps, stale lead detection, and short drafts. bob should not
feel like an AI wrapper.

## visual direction

bob is dark-first, minimal, and composed.

The interface should use:

- black and near-black surfaces
- restrained borders and shadows
- clear typography
- small warm accents
- dense but readable layouts
- simple states that do not compete for attention

The product should avoid:

- loud gradients
- childish illustration
- oversized marketing language
- crowded dashboards
- decorative UI that does not clarify the workflow

The visual tone should sit near modern product tools: quiet, precise, and
confident. It should still feel personal through copy, spacing, and small brand
details.

## bob mascot direction

bob can carry a subtle mascot idea: a blonde cocker spaniel presence.

This should be treated as brand atmosphere, not a cartoon character. The mascot
direction is calm, observant, warm, and premium.

Use the idea sparingly:

- a small warm accent color inspired by blonde fur
- soft naming around quiet attention and companionship
- optional future imagery that feels editorial or product-led
- small brand details that make bob memorable without distracting from the app

Avoid:

- childish dog icons
- paw prints
- jokes or pet puns
- exaggerated expressions
- mascot-led onboarding
- anything that makes the product feel unserious

The best version of bob feels like a quiet presence in the room: attentive,
reliable, and never demanding attention.
